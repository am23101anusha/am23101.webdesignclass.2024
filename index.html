My Research in VR Telerehabilitation

Aim of my research: To develop an efficient telerehabilitation system using Virtual Reality (VR), the Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU), and Electromyography (EMG) that considers patient motivation, and provides easy data transmission and feedback.

With the rising advancement of technology, Virtual reality is a new approach to the healthcare field. With the help of VR, it is now possible to provide telerehabilitation services to patients from their homes my research exclusively focuses on people who are affected by stroke and have limited motor function in their upper limbs. Currently, I am using an Oculus Quest 2 to build my first version of the game and I plan to incorporate it with an Inertial Measurement Unit Sensor (IMU).

Currently, I am working on collecting data related to upper limb movements using MAC 3D system.. This system has several cameras to track and calculate the joint angles and positions of the body parts we want to measure. I am also learning how to use the Inertial Measurement Unit simultaneously, which is most commonly used for hand-tracking movements.

By the end of my undergraduate program, I hope to come up with an effective system that is considerate of patients and provides an improvement in upper limb motor function.
